Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore

Tagore was a polymath, poet, musician, and artist from the India. He reshaped Bengali literature and music, as well as Indian art with Contextual Modernism in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. I 1913 the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.Tagore's poetic songs were viewed as spiritual and mercurial; however, his "elegant prose and magical poetry" remain largely unknown outside Bengal. He is referred to as "the Bard of Bengal".

Timeline

  • 1861-Born in Calcutta, India
  • 1868-Wrote his first poem when he was just 7 years
  • 1878-Tagore's first book of poetries appeared
  • 1883-Got married to Mrinalini Devi
  • 1901-Founded school at Shanti Niketan
  • 1910-Wrote Gitanjali in Bengali
  • 1911 -Wrote Jana Gana Mana which later became our National Anthem
  • 1913-Awarded Nobel prize in literature
  • 1924-Tagore opened Vishva Bharati University at Shanti Niketan
  • 1942-Rabindranath Tagore died in Calcutta, India

Where The Mind Is Without Fear

"Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high
Where knowledge is free
Where the world has not been broken up into fragments
By narrow domestic walls
Where words come out from the depth of truth
Where tireless striving stretches its arms towards perfection
Where the clear stream of reason has not lost its way
Into the dreary desert sand of dead habit
Where the mind is led forward by thee
Into ever-widening thought and action
Into that heaven of freedom, my Father, let my country awake."
